Case Study 1: Creating Inclusive Emotion Recognition Materials

Context & Goal

Develop a set of body and face emotion recognition stimuli that are representative, inclusive, and valid for both autistic and non-autistic audiences. Existing materials lacked diversity in neurotype, gender, and age, limiting ecological validity.

Your Role

Lead researcher — end-to-end project ownership, from design to validation.

Methods

  • Stakeholder interviews with autistic community representatives and psychologists
  • Iterative testing with pilot groups
  • Filming, editing, and psychometric validation of visual stimuli

Process

  1. Conducted needs assessment to identify representation gaps
  2. Recruited 16 diverse actors (half autistic, gender-balanced, age-diverse, including non-binary identities)
  3. Produced both static and dynamic emotion expressions
  4. Ran multiple validation studies (~200 participants per round) with statistical analysis in R and SPSS

Findings & Insights

  • Inclusion of autistic actors increased perceived relevance for neurodiverse users
  • Dynamic stimuli improved recognition accuracy for certain emotions

Impact

  • Open-access resource adopted by other researchers and practitioners
  • Secured £5k competitive funding (Impact Accelerator Fund)

Case Study 2: Evaluating Educational Resources in Chaotic, Real-World Environments

Context & Goal

Assess how children and parents engaged with cognitive testing activities during Summer Scientist Week, a busy public science event. The challenge was to maintain usability and engagement in a high-distraction environment.

Your Role

Lead UX researcher — experimental design, field testing, and evaluation.

Methods

  • Contextual inquiry in event space
  • Direct observation and behaviour logging
  • Post-activity surveys with parents and children

Process

  1. Designed a child-friendly cognitive test with visual prompts and timed responses
  2. Managed and trained research assistants to ensure consistent data collection
  3. Collected and analysed data across three years to track trends

Findings & Insights

  • Simplified instructions and visual cues boosted engagement in children under 8
  • Queue length and noise levels directly impacted performance for younger participants

Impact

  • Testing station layout redesigned based on findings
  • Increased completion rates and parental satisfaction year-on-year

Case Study 3: Improving Survey Data Quality in Educational Settings

Context & Goal

Improve data quality and completion rates for school-administered surveys in emotion recognition research. Initial completion was low due to unclear instructions and survey fatigue.

Your Role

Lead researcher — survey design, pilot testing, and stakeholder engagement.

Methods

  • Think-aloud usability testing with teachers
  • Quantitative tracking of completion and error rates
  • Iterative survey refinement in Qualtrics

Process

  1. Identified pain points through think-aloud sessions
  2. Reduced redundancy and restructured question flow
  3. Added progress bars, chunked questions, and embedded micro-instructions

Findings & Insights

  • Completion rates increased by 25%
  • Error rates dropped significantly on critical questions

Impact

  • Positive feedback from school staff on usability
  • Survey design became the departmental standard for related projects

Case Study 4: Optimising Online Statistical Learning Resources in a Constrained Platform

Context & Goal

Enhance engagement and usability of R programming learning materials on the Maths and Stats Help (MASH) website, hosted on LibGuides — a fixed-template platform with major design constraints.

Your Role

Digital learning and UX researcher — resource development, analytics tracking, and optimisation.

Methods

  • Web analytics review (page views, bounce rates, time-on-page)
  • User navigation path analysis
  • Content restructuring and iterative testing

Process

  1. Analysed baseline metrics to identify low-engagement sections
  2. Created new R guides with code examples, downloadable resources, and step-by-step explanations
  3. Improved navigation and content hierarchy within LibGuides’ limitations through internal linking and clear section headings
  4. Monitored post-launch engagement and refined content based on behaviour patterns

Findings & Insights

  • Example-driven pages had higher engagement and lower bounce rates
  • Strategic internal linking reduced navigation dead ends

Impact

  • Increased time-on-page by over 40% for core R guide sections
  • R materials became one of the most visited parts of the MASH site
  • Provided a reusable template for other statistical learning resources

Note: Work executed within LibGuides’ fixed template; no template changes were possible.
